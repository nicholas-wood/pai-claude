Hi all,
 
Planning for LIMSOC next season has commenced. We are putting together some SME groups to have input on some of the major projects we are considering for next season, and I'd like to invite you to put your hand up for any you would be interested in contributing to.
 
A bit of context on how these groups work.
 
We are doing the planning for all the major projects now, even though we may not end up delivering all of them this season.
Each major project will look to bring together a group of SMEs to provide input. Not every project needs one, so this is focused on the major pieces.
These groups are informal and sit outside our formal governance structure. Their role is to provide input, not to make decisions, which stay where they do now.
The time commitment will vary by project, but would typically involve reviewing the project brief, providing iterative feedback, and helping with user testing and rollout considerations.
 
The four groups we are starting with are below. Have a read and let me know which one, or ones, you would be interested in.
 
Incident Categorisation and Flow
This group covers how incidents are classified and how they move through LIMSOC from creation to resolution. The work includes moving to a clearer categorisation model that guides operators to a single correct classification, simplifying incident creation so it is faster under pressure, and tidying up how supporting information and labels are captured. The aim is more consistent data and an incident management experience that is quicker to start and easier to maintain.
 
Call Out Systems and Capabilities
This group looks at how we coordinate callouts and, just as importantly, how we get the right information to the members responding. That includes moving beyond a purely unit-based model towards geographical callout, using live location and member qualifications to reach nearby available responders based on proximity and capability rather than unit affiliation alone. It also covers giving responding members visibility of key incident information, such as the incident brief and responding services, rather than relying on radio traffic alone, so they arrive with situational awareness rather than starting from zero.
 
Rostering within LIMSOC
This group covers bringing volunteer rostering into LIMSOC as an integrated function, rather than managing it through spreadsheets and external tools. The work spans team-based rostering, more flexible models such as shift bidding, unit-level administration and notifications, and simple measures like sign-off reminders that keep our operational data accurate. The intent is a single platform for the volunteer's operational life, with rostering connected to sign-on, notifications, and reporting.
 
AI within our Incident Management Practice
This group explores where AI can genuinely support our incident management, in targeted and high-value ways. Examples include an AI generated situational brief that keeps a running summary of an evolving incident, prompts that catch information recorded in the log but not yet actioned, and validation that flags gaps or inconsistencies on incident and first aid records before they become the permanent record. The focus is on reducing cognitive load and improving data quality, while keeping operational judgement firmly with our people.
 
If you would like to be involved, just reply with the group or groups you would like to join and I'll follow up with the detail and next steps. Feel free to flag anywhere you have particular experience or interest.
