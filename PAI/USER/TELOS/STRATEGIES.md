# Strategies

> How you approach your missions, goals, and problems — the playbook.

- **S0: Lead with leverage.** Evaluate decisions through impact, leverage, and optionality; bias toward the few actions that move the most. Impact through leverage, not effort.
- **S1: Engineering as the differentiator.** Pair deep technical depth with strategic and executive communication. The T-shaped combination is the edge, so never drop either side; foreground engineering as a strength, not baggage.
- **S2: Build over ask for reversible actions.** Execute low-risk, reversible moves directly and keep momentum; reserve deliberation and approval for the irreversible or high-stakes.
- **S3: First principles, then pragmatism.** Reason from fundamentals, then ship pragmatic outcomes over theory. Strong opinions, loosely held.
- **S4: Precision over vagueness.** Recommendation first, then the reasoning. Executive-ready artefacts, no hand-waving.
- **S5: AI as the multiplier.** Use PAI and a company of agents to 100x output across every domain (M0), and operate publicly as an AI-native leader (M1).

## Notes

Strategies are the *how* that connects goals to missions. A strategy is durable across specific goals — if you changed all your goals, your strategies might still apply.
