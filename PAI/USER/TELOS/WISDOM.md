# Wisdom

> Hard-won insights you keep coming back to - the distilled output of years of living. The filter the DA runs recommendations through.

- People have to earn the right to complain. Earn it by at least trying to solve it.
- Strong opinions, loosely held.
- The future will favour the wide over the narrow. Keep a portfolio of interests.
- Superpowers are extra superpowers when you take them somewhere new. In your current environment everyone has them; in a new one they are a massive differentiator.
- Everything you want is downstream. Fight for things, but don't swim upstream. If it feels like swimming upstream, the cost is not worth it.
- Do the verb rather than trying to be the noun.
- A walk may or may not solve your problems, but it definitely won't make them worse.
- Writing things down isn't just for communicating to others. It sounds out and completes the idea for you.
- The most interesting people in the room are the most interested people in the room.
- Ask questions that throw people off autopilot.
- Build. Teach. Lead.
- Care. Continuous asking, responding, and evaluating. Ask the question every day when you log in.
- Community is a group of people who choose to grow together.
- You will know you lived a life worth living if people pick up and keep going with what you were doing.
- Before you try to change the world, let the world change you. Take input.
- The truth is more important than not stepping on toes.
- Don't be close with anyone unless they will say something in public that costs them. Are they authentic? Will they stand up for what they believe?
- The receipts for love are grief and anxiety.
- Sell yourself long; assume you are selling yourself short. Want to punch above your weight? Get a big spoon and get ready to eat shit.
- People are losing the skill of rejection. Resilience to "no" is a muscle.
- Great minds don't think alike.
- It is supposed to be fun. It is supposed to be a game.
