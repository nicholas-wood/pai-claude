# Narratives

> The elevator-pitch versions of your mission and beliefs - the framings you use in a profile, a first meeting, or a pitch. Built from your LinkedIn bio and your 2026 application cover letters, so this is your own voice.

## Core positioning

- **N0:** A product-focused engineer working across technology, product, and strategy to deliver outcomes that hold up in the real world. Increasingly drawn to problems that need more than technical delivery: bringing structure, alignment, and clear decision-making to complex environments with multiple stakeholders and competing priorities, including direct engagement with executive and C-suite stakeholders.
- **N1 (the thesis):** As technology becomes more accessible with AI, the value is shifting from the ability to build to the ability to understand deeply and communicate clearly to any audience. That is where I excel, and it is a capability that only becomes more valuable.
- **N2 (the bridge):** My career started in technology, architecting and delivering platforms across regulated banking, defence, and government. What I have consistently been drawn to is the broader problem: framing what should be built and why, aligning competing stakeholders, and distilling complex needs into strategy that can be executed. I want to apply that across a wider range of industries and problems.
- **N3 (one-liner):** I turn complex technical problems into clear decisions, because I have been both the one building it and the one presenting it to the board.

## Character

- **N4:** Strong opinions, loosely held. I form clear views and contribute actively, but if there is a better idea I want to find it. I love nothing more than being proven wrong with a better one and jumping on board immediately.
- **N5:** Most engaged when the problem is difficult, the path is not obvious, and the work requires both depth and breadth to get right.
- **N6:** Self-directed. The work I am proudest of was never asked of me. I sought it out because the problems were interesting and the impact was tangible.

## Proof points (the evidence the narratives rest on)

- Directed the national scaling strategy for a 199,000-member, $100M, 315-club federated organisation (Life Saving Victoria / LIMSOC): designed the operating model, governance framework, and commercial structure from a blank page, and presented to C-suite leadership.
- Chaired a national policy rewrite across 7 to 8 state bodies, now board-endorsed and in effect across Australia, adopted by schools and emergency service providers.
- Built and launched my own SaaS product from problem identification to a live platform with real users, using AI-native development (Claude Code) to operate as a solo founder at the pace of a team. A second is underway.
- Operating range: seven years from engineering teams to executive stakeholders, across banking (ANZ, Macquarie), defence and government (Leidos), and emergency services (LSV / SLSA).
