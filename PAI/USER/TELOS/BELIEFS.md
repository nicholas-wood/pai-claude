# Beliefs

> What you hold true about the world - axioms that shape your decisions.

## Core

- **Strong Opinions Loosely Held** - Have a position, defend it, but update it when evidence warrants.
- **Pragmatism over theory** - What works matters more than what's elegant.
- **Impact through leverage** - Seek maximum impact per unit of effort.
- **Engineering is a superpower in strategy** - People who can build AND think strategically are rare and valuable.
- **Precision matters** - Vague thinking produces vague outcomes.

## Work

- First-principles thinking beats pattern-matching.
- The best decisions are framed as trade-offs, not right/wrong.
- Titles are signals, but capability is substance.
- Trust is built by competence and reliability, not by words.
- Complexity should be earned, not assumed.

## Decision-making

- Evaluate through: impact, leverage, optionality.
- Distinguish risk from uncertainty. Manage what you can measure.
- Over-analysis is a real cost. Sometimes the best move is the next move.
- Pushback is a feature, not a threat.

## Life

- Operational credibility matters. Do the work, not just the thinking about the work.
- Community service (surf lifesaving) is a non-negotiable part of identity.
- Career should compound, not just progress.

## Technology and AI

- AI won't take your job; someone who can use AI will.
- As technology becomes more accessible, value shifts from the ability to build to the ability to understand deeply and communicate clearly to any audience.
- One AI learns it, they all learn it - and they all make the same mistake. Human society is resilient because we hold diverse skills and different failure modes; converging AI does not.
- AI is like transportation: a scooter and a rocket are both transport, suited to different needs. People are starstruck by the rockets.
- Brace for enshittification. The VC honeymoon ends; once the billions must be repaid, they monetise, and it is a collective-action problem (one descends, all descend).
- Storytelling is the key innovator skill. Promise runs ahead of performance to access capital and pull the future forward (Musk, Tesla, SpaceX valuations).
- Above 2-3% of GDP, a technology almost always crashes first (railroads, electrification, internet). AI is not guaranteed to succeed, and AI may largely replace AI.
- Human error and imperfection are what we value: handmade goods, chess masters over engines, books with typos.
- Tech CEOs optimise for money, not for you. Trust should come from legislators, not from hoping executives behave.

---
*Beliefs differ from opinions: a belief frames everything downstream. The DA uses these to understand why you react certain ways to suggestions.*
