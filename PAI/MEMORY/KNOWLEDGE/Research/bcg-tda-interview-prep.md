# BCG TDA Interview Prep -- Master Document

## Summary

Comprehensive interview preparation system for Nick's BCG Tech and Data Advantage (TDA) talent screen (Friday 2026-05-29, 30 min Zoom). Referred by Trini Bigalow and Nora Hocke (both BCG Principals). The document covers: a full question bank with drafted answers, a 30-minute call agenda map, a 36-hour prep plan (Thursday + Friday morning), a 20-session case prep plan over two weeks, TDA-specific tailoring, and a coach-gap analysis. Companion artefacts: BCG_TDA_Story_Bank.md (STAR+R behavioural stories), BCG_TDA_Daily_Worksheets.docx, BCG_TDA_Friday_OnePager.docx.

## Key Facts

**Question bank (20 questions):** CV walk-through (90 sec draft provided), Why consulting, Why BCG, Why TDA, Why now, Level/compensation, Strengths (top 3), Development areas, and behaviourals I-P (Leadership through ambiguity, Failure, Conflict, Persuasion, Tech translation, Ethical judgment, Delivering through resistance, High-stakes technical decision).

**Drafted answers for key questions:**
- CV walk-through (tightened, ~290 words, ~1:50): defence/intelligence/government/banking arc, solo SaaS (AccessBuddy), national policy work at SLSA, Terrigal workshop scene as Trojan horse for M story, closes with "strong opinions, loosely held / want to be the dumbest person in the room"
- Why TDA: "I don't have to choose" between technology and strategy; engineering stays, the centre of gravity shifts
- Why now: three reasons -- generative AI, right career stage, love of the work
- Development area: conviction landing on others; framed as interpersonal effect (EQ), not inability to bring people along

**TDA tailoring:**
- Cases bias technical: data platform investment, build vs buy, AI use case prioritisation, tech function transformation, digital transformation diagnostic, data monetisation
- Phrase: "I can read the architecture; I'm here to make the commercial call"
- Most-tested behavioural: M (tech translation -- SLSA Terrigal workshop)
- TDA-specific phrasing (use sparingly): "technology as a source of competitive advantage", "AI at scale", "the data foundation that the use cases sit on top of", "build, buy, partner", "value at stake"
- Red flags: sounding like a developer at BCG; over-engineering case answers; implying tech firms are better at tech

**20-session case prep plan:**
- 10 working days, 2 sessions/day (AM + PM), ~50 min each
- Each session: mental math (10 min), market sizing or issue tree (10 min), case work (20 min), fit story or reading synthesis (10 min), self-review (2 min)
- Week 1: foundations (opener drills, profitability, market entry, pricing, M&A)
- Week 2: TDA archetypes (build vs buy, data platform investment, AI use case prioritisation, Casey chatbot, full mocks)
- Paid coaching recommended: after Session 10 and after Session 18 (~$250-300 each, PrepLounge or Management Consulted, ex-MBB with TDA/digital experience)

**Closing question (highest-leverage):** "Before we wrap, is there anything from our conversation today that would give you hesitation about progressing me to the next round? I'd rather address it now."

**Key referrers:** Trini Bigalow ("there is no traditional consulting background"), Nora Hocke (TDA, hosts Fintech Files podcast for BCG Platinion)
