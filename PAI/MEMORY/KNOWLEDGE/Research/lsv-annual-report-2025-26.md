# LSV State Lifesaving Communications Officer -- Annual Report 2025-26

## Summary

Nick's annual report to the Lifesaving Operations Council (LSOC) as State Lifesaving Communications Officer for the 2025-26 season. The report covers outcomes delivered across radio and LIMSOC, season statistics, and priorities for 2026-27. Written in collective voice ("we / the team") with humility rather than self-promotion. Delivered to LSOC as part of the standard officer reporting cycle.

## Key Facts

**Season statistics:**
- 674 incidents managed through LIMSOC
- 1,330 first aid forms recorded, including 241 major first aids

**Radio accomplishments:**
- Statewide callsign refresh -- clearer, more concise callsigns
- Motorola Wave PTX app trial -- radio channels to duty officers' phones
- Coverage upgrades at five clubs (closed long-standing black spots)
- Full statewide radio audit (validated picture of every asset)
- Next-generation radio requirements gathered
- New LSV Comms processes established
- Ambulance Victoria added to LSV channels for the first time

**LIMSOC accomplishments:**
- Industry-leading first aid form and new/updated IRL logs
- Digitised medication register
- Statewide visibility uplift for Comms and Duty Officers
- Field Officer view upgrades
- Live RPAS footage streaming into LIMSOC (with AI analysis/enrichment pipeline)
- Frontline map capability built, tested, and ready to launch
- Major reporting uplift (dashboards, PDF reports, data sharing)
- Refreshed notifications system

**Priorities for 2026-27:**
- Radio: next-generation device scoping; multi-agency interoperability; coverage gap closure
- LIMSOC -- four focus areas:
  - Enhance incident management (simpler, faster, more accurate capture)
  - Get more information to frontline (launch frontline map, RPAS, patrol-member situational awareness)
  - Rostering as integrated functionality
  - AI innovation (reduce admin load, surface right information at right time)
