# SMLSC Clubhouse Renewal -- Board Proposal and VESEP Funding Model

## Summary

Nick's board proposal (dated 2026-05-19) seeking endorsement of the SMLSC Clubhouse Renewal Project as a single coordinated program of works, approval to prepare a VESEP funding application, and approval of the club contribution funding model. The paper is a direction-and-model endorsement, not a final dollar commitment; firm quotes and LSV endorsement return to the board before any binding commitment. Nick's framing line: "from 'wow, there is crap everywhere' to 'wow, this is an impressive clubhouse.'"

## Key Facts

**The motion (four-part resolution):**
1. Endorses the Clubhouse Renewal Project as a single coordinated project
2. Approves preparation of a VESEP funding application for the full scope
3. Approves the funding model: term deposit drawn down at maturity for the club's one-third share, fully repaid from 50% of net functions revenue (estimated under six months at current performance)
4. Authorises the sponsor (Nick) to obtain firm quotes and engage LSV on VESEP endorsement, returning with final figures before binding commitment

**VESEP funding structure:**
- Victorian Emergency Services Equipment Program: 2:1 government-to-club contribution ratio
- Club share = one-third of project total
- Club share funded from existing term deposit (drawn at maturity), repaid from 50% of net functions revenue
- No impact on operating cash flow; no draw on member levies; no debt
- Repayment estimated under six months at current functions performance
- Trade rationale: implicit return from the project (OH&S risks closed, function revenue capacity unlocked, member engagement) materially exceeds the cash-rate return the term deposit would otherwise earn

**Why now (four drivers):**
1. VESEP round: act in current cycle or miss the window
2. Functions revenue is at a high (can underwrite the club's share)
3. Sequencing leverage: shared trades make a single program cheaper than years of incremental patches
4. Member engagement: clubhouse either supports the strategic plan or quietly undermines it

**Project scope (organised by area):**
- Operational wing: office door (OFF-01), security cupboard (OFF-03), hallway cupboards (HAL-01), drawer dishwasher (KIT-01), extra kitchenette storage (KIT-02), washing machine plumbing (CLN-01), patrol tower storage and cable management (PT-01), PA microphone (PA-01), CCTV (SEC-01), keyless entry 10 points (SEC-02), front door keypad (SEC-03), booking/access API integration (SEC-04), wetsuit racks (CHG-01), code lockers (CHG-02)
- Function room: acoustic panelling (FB-01), teleconference (FB-02), additional storage (FB-03), secure lock (FB-04), anti-glare display (FB-05), memorabilia framing (FB-06), tunable lighting (FB-07), magnetic door stops (BLD-01)
- Gear shed (largest opportunity): bi-directional access control (GS-01), full reconfiguration -- two dividing fences, operational/competition zone split, ski rack relocation (GS-02), two custom mobile rescue board racks with IRB slide-tilt sled mechanism (GS-03)

**GS-03 (custom board racks):** designed to roll into the central accessway when needed, opening a flexible training / fitness space in the NE quadrant; IRBs loaded and unloaded at safe handling height via slide-then-tilt sled.

**Next steps (as of 2026-05-19):**
1. Board endorsement
2. Engage LSV on VESEP endorsement pathway and round timing (within 2 weeks of endorsement)
3. Finalise board rack technical drawing pack (NV, Club Captain)
4. Obtain firm quotes for all items (within 6 weeks of endorsement)
5. Prepare VESEP application package
6. Return to board with final figures before submission
