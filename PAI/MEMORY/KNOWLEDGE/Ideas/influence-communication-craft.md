# Influence & Communication Craft

**Type:** Idea / playbook
**Owner:** Nick

A working playbook of influence and communication techniques Nick collects and applies, especially in stakeholder and executive settings.

## Techniques

- **Remembering names** - listen, repeat, reply. People forget to listen because they are rehearsing their reply (you can't read and write at once). Repeat the name twice, clarify spelling ("Stephen with a v or a ph?"), and associate it with a story or someone you know.
- **Always wrap things in a story** - it does not matter what you say, it matters what they remember and what they retell.
- **Focus follows yours** - people look where you look. Glance at your watch and that becomes their focus.
- **Resonate, don't direct** - get into their river of thought and flow with them first, then guide.
- **Call out the script to weaken it** - say the quiet part out loud ("that is a firm handshake - I read that only alpha males do that"). Surfacing what is running in someone's head lets the conversation break away from it, and hands you more control.
- **Context plus permission** - the right context makes a decision automatic (standing in a shower gives permission to undress; the hypnotised-sheriff story). Ask: in what context would the decision I need this person to make become an automatic thing?
- **Make them feel clever** - present two familiar pieces of information and let them link it themselves. An idea that comes from their own mind is impossible to resist (the news-headline trick: state the facts, let the brain conclude).
- **Frame meetings with outcomes** - framing is perception.
- **Negative dissociation** - get agreement on who someone is, not just how they will act. Prompt an "I am" in their head.
- **The authenticity test** - real trust comes from people willing to say something in public that costs them.
