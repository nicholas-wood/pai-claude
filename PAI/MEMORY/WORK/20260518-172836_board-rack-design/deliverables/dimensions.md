# Custom Board Rack — Technical Specification

**Quantity:** 2 units (identical)
**Application:** Surf life saving club — rescue board storage with integrated IRB cradle
**Document purpose:** Fabricator quote pack

## 2. Board Storage

**Per shelf:** 2 rescue boards side-by-side, slid in from either end.

**Board envelope per slot:** 3,220 mm (L) × 600 mm (W)
- Board: 3,200 mm × 580 mm
- 1 mm padding all around: 3,220 mm × 582 mm (rounded to 600 mm for slot width)
- Inter-board centre gap: 20 mm (2× 10 mm padding)

**Shelf levels (resting surface height above floor):**

| Level | Height (mm) | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (lowest) | 300 | Clears 250 mm fin to floor |
| 2 | 700 | +400 mm |
| 3 | 1,100 | +400 mm |
| 4 | 1,500 | +400 mm |
| 5 (top) | 1,900 | +400 mm; 300 mm to top of frame at 2,200 mm |

**Shelf supports per level:** 2 × cross-piece (perpendicular to board length).
- Cross-piece A: 600 mm from one end
- Cross-piece B: 600 mm from opposite end

- Cross-piece section: 40 × 40 × 2 mm MS SHS, hot-dip galvanised
- Cross-piece span: 1,220 mm (full internal width)
- Padding: 10 mm closed-cell EVA foam, marine-grade adhesive, glued to top contact surface

## 3. Structural Frame

### Uprights (8 total)
- Section: 50 × 50 × 3 mm MS SHS, hot-dip galvanised
- Height: 2,200 mm from floor (welded to caster mounting plate at base, to top frame at top)
- Placement: 4 per long side, spaced equally (centres at 0, 1,033, 2,067, 3,100 mm from front)
- All on outside of rack — no uprights at short ends

### Long-side bracing
- Diagonal X-braces between adjacent uprights, both long sides
- Section: 25 × 25 × 2 mm MS SHS
- Pattern: full X between every adjacent upright pair (3 bays × 2 sides = 6 X-braces)
- Bracing kept BELOW lowest shelf and ABOVE highest shelf to leave shelf area clear

### Top frame
- Closed rectangular box welded to upright tops
- Members: 50 × 50 × 3 mm MS SHS on perimeter + 2× 50 × 25 × 3 mm RHS slide rails along long sides
- Provides lateral stiffness and supports sled slide-rail system

### End openings
- Both short ends are entirely open — no end frames, no end uprights
- Boards slide in/out from either end

## 4. Mobility (Casters)

- **Quantity:** 4
- **Type:** Industrial swivel caster with locking brake (top-lock)
- **Wheel diameter:** 150 mm
- **Wheel material:** Polyurethane tread on cast iron centre (or equivalent marine-grade)
- **Top plate:** 100 × 100 mm, 4-bolt mount
- **Working load limit:** 250 kg per caster (1,000 kg total — 2× safety on full load)
- **Mounted height (overall):** ≤ 200 mm (wheel + plate)
- **Position:** Bolted to underside of bottom rail at each corner of the rack

**Note:** Bottom-most shelf at 300 mm allows 100 mm air gap above caster top plate.

## 5. Slide-Then-Tilt Sled Mechanism

### Slide rails (2× per rack)
- Section: 50 × 25 × 3 mm RHS, hot-dip galvanised, hardened bearing surface or PE strip on top
- Length: 3,220 mm (full rack length)
- Position: along the inside top edge of each long-side upright row, top surface at 2,150 mm above floor (so sled top sits at 2,200 mm including roller + sled section thickness)
- Termination at rear: each rail ends in a pivot bracket (downward-facing concave cradle for rear roller)

### Sleds (2× per rack)
- Section: 50 × 50 × 3 mm SHS top member with 25 mm EVA-padded HDPE strip on top (IRB contact)
- Length: 3,000 mm each
- Spacing: 900 mm centre-to-centre (sleds 450 mm from rack centreline each)
- Forward portion (forward of pivot, on rack top in slid position): 600 mm
- Rearward portion (rearward of pivot, cantilever in slid position): 2,400 mm

### Crossbars (linking the two sleds)
- **Front crossbar:** 40 × 40 × 3 mm SHS, length 900 mm, welded between sled forward tips. Carries winch cable attachment eye (centred).
- **Rear crossbar:** 40 × 40 × 3 mm SHS, length 900 mm, welded at rear-roller position. Carries IRB bow fastening point (centred D-ring or shackle).

### Rollers (4 per sled assembly = 8 total per rack)
- **Front rollers (2):** nylon, 50 mm diameter, on M16 stainless steel axle, mounted at sled forward tip (port + starboard)
- **Rear rollers (2):** nylon, 50 mm diameter, on M16 stainless steel axle, mounted 2,400 mm rearward of forward rollers (port + starboard) — these become the pivot when slid
- Rollers must allow rotation about their axles (slide motion) AND allow the rear rollers to seat in the pivot brackets

### Slide latch (port + starboard)
- Sprung pin or detent latch holding sled in stowed position
- Manual release lever accessible from rack rear
- Positive engagement — prevents accidental slide during transit
- Secondary safety: webbing strap from front crossbar to rack top frame (belt-and-braces)

### Pivot brackets (2× per rack — one each side, at rail rear)
- Section: 6 mm MS plate, formed into downward concave cradle to receive rear roller
- Once rear roller is seated, the sled is constrained to rotate about that axis
- The sled lifts OFF the rails for free tilt; rollers do not interfere

### Stabilizer leg (1× per rack)
- Drop-down leg at rack rear, hinged at bottom rail
- Section: 40 × 40 × 3 mm SHS
- Length: 300 mm (when deployed, leg foot rests on ground)
- Adjustable foot pad with cup or rubber base
- **Must be deployed before sliding the sled rearward** (operating procedure)
- Stowed: hooked up against the bottom rail with a clip

## 6. Winch System

- **Type:** 12 V DC electric winch, drum type, with automatic load brake
- **Working load limit (WLL):** 1,000 kg minimum
- **Drum capacity:** ≥ 8 m of 6 mm stainless steel cable
- **Mount location:** Front-base of rack — bolted to bottom-rail front face, centred between casters
- **Power:** Quick-connect Anderson plug, run from external 12 V deep-cycle or vehicle battery
- **Control:** Hand-held remote on 5 m cable; in-line emergency stop

### Cable routing

```
Winch (front-base of rack, x=0, h≈300mm)
   |
   | (cable angles up and rearward)
   |
   v
Front crossbar of sled (when stowed: x≈2,620mm, h=2,200mm)
```

The cable runs from the winch DC up-and-rearward to the sled's front crossbar. This routing provides the correct counter-clockwise rotational moment to raise the sled rear back to horizontal at all tilt angles (verified by first-principles geometry analysis — see PRD).

Cable length range:
- Sled stowed (or slid, sled horizontal): ≈ 3,200 mm
- Sled tilted 40°: ≈ 3,600 mm
- Cable pays out ≈ 400 mm during deployment

Maximum cable tension (computed from moment balance, 150 kg IRB + sled cantilever):
- At 0° tilt (worst case): ≈ 640 kgf
- At 40° tilt: ≈ 290 kgf
- 1,000 kg WLL winch provides 1.5× safety at the worst case.

## 7. IRB Fastening

- **Bow strap:** 50 mm marine-grade webbing with cam-buckle or ratchet
- **Anchor on rack side:** D-ring or stainless shackle, welded eye on the rear crossbar
- **Anchor on IRB side:** existing bow towing eye
- **Length:** sized to permit deployment from horizontal to fully tilted
- **Failure mode:** strap MUST hold the IRB during winch failure — rated 2,000 kg break strength

---

## 8. Operating Sequence

### To deploy IRB (from stowed inside shed):
1. Roll rack out through shed door (sleds stowed, top of rack at 2,200 mm)
2. Position rack with rear facing the unloading area
3. **Deploy stabilizer leg** at rack rear (drop down, lock in place)
4. Release slide latch (port + starboard)
5. Pull sled assembly rearward by ≈ 820 mm until rear rollers seat in pivot brackets (audible/visual confirmation of seat)
6. Confirm IRB bow strap is connected from IRB bow to rear crossbar
7. Operate winch — let cable out slowly; sled tilts rearward under gravity
8. Stop tilting at desired height for IRB lift-off (typically 30°–45°)
9. Crew lifts IRB from sled, releases bow strap
10. Sled is now empty — winch cable still attached to front crossbar

### To stow IRB (reverse):
1. Sled empty and tilted at deployment angle
2. Position IRB on sled at ground end, secure bow strap to rear crossbar
3. Operate winch — pull cable in; sled rotates back toward horizontal, lifting IRB
4. Once sled reaches horizontal, push sled assembly forward (≈ 820 mm) until front rollers re-engage rails
5. Engage slide latch (port + starboard) — confirm positive lock
6. Stow stabilizer leg (clip up under bottom rail)
7. Roll rack back into shed

## 10. Fabrication Notes

- **All welds:** 6 mm continuous fillet welds unless noted; full-penetration butt welds at high-load joints (pivot bracket to top frame, winch mount to bottom rail)
- **Galvanising:** All steelwork after fabrication; provide vent and drain holes per AS/NZS 4680
- **Tolerances:** ± 2 mm on overall dimensions; ± 0.5 mm on roller/rail interface
- **Painting (optional):** Powder-coat over galvanising in club colours, exterior-grade
- **Edge treatment:** Grind all sharp edges, especially at end-load openings where boards slide
- **Quality:** Test-fit sled slide and pivot engagement before delivery; demonstrate winch operation
- **Quantity:** 2 identical units
- **Delivery:** Fully assembled OR knocked-down with bolt-up joints if access constrained — fabricator's option
