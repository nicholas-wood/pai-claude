# Board questions on the IMS Discovery Update

**To:** [Director]
**From:** Nick Wood, on behalf of LSV
**Subject:** Board questions on the IMS Discovery Update

Based on the information and updates provided to the Lifesaving Systems Working Group (LSWG) and ICT Working Group (ICTWG), our view is that the Discovery Update report as presented is incomplete, imbalanced, and at points lacks the integrity required for a decision of this scale. Six questions follow that we believe most useful at board level. Each should be cross-checked against the final Board Report (V1.2) before being raised, in case any have been addressed in the version the board has received.

## 1. The functional assessment was done against a different solution

The 12 months of functional assessment by our lifesaving community was conducted against WebEOC plus Respond52. The current recommendation is WebEOC alone. If WebEOC alone had been the option, LSAG would have had a clear winner on functional grounds, which would not have been webeoc, and a Meshn assessment would not have been required. On what basis can the existing assessment be carried forward to support a recommendation for a solution it did not actually evaluate?

## 2. Respond52 was only removed after LSV prompted Meshn to ask the vendor

Respond52 was confirmed out of scope by Juvare in writing on 21 April, one day after LSV's questions were submitted, and only after LSV's questions caused Meshn to seek clarification from the vendor. How was this not identified earlier in the process, given Respond52 was explicitly referenced in the original update to LSAG and the ICTWG? On that note, why was there an odd number of vendor workshop sessions across the assessment, rather than a comparable number for each vendor?

## 3. Frontline members have been removed from the recommendation

With Respond52 removed, the recommended solution no longer addresses our frontline operational members. The frontline experience was flagged by lifesaving subject-matter experts in the LSWG as an essential consideration and the Ops App issues have been the primary concern flagged. Why is the recommendation proceeding without an answer for that group, and what is the substitute pathway for frontline functionality if it is not Respond52, the SLSA Operations App, or LIMSOC?

## 4. The Ops App liability is not presented as a downside of the recommendation

Under the recommended pathway, the SLSA Operations App must continue to operate, be maintained, and be integrated with WebEOC. That is ongoing investment in a system already considered outdated and underperforming. Under LIMSOC, the Ops App can be retired. Why is the cost and risk of keeping the Ops App alive, including continued spend on a system the organisation already considers end-of-life, not presented as a downside of the recommended solution?

## 5. The strategic loss of autonomy is not weighed

SLSA's strategic plan is centred on innovation and technology. The recommendation ties the organisation to a large external vendor where we will compete for product attention with hundreds of larger customers. SLSA was recently affected by exactly this dependency in the sport space. At the same time, generative AI is materially reducing the cost of bespoke development. Has the board paper assessed the strategic cost of giving up autonomy over our own platform, and the opportunity cost of choosing a vendor-dependent path at the very moment that the economics of bespoke development are moving in the other direction?

## 6. The five-year feature-complete timeline has not been reconciled

When this was presented to the board 12 months ago, WebEOC indicated a five-year path to feature completeness against our requirements. The current paper presents a 12-month implementation window. Has the underlying timeline genuinely changed, or has the scope been reduced? Has the opportunity cost of waiting up to five years simply to reach our current capability, before considering any of the new capabilities states want to leverage, been quantified anywhere in the analysis?

## Verification points for the board paper

Before the questions above are raised, please cross-check the final Board Report (V1.2) for whether each of the following has been addressed:

- Carry-forward of the functional assessment from WebEOC + Respond52 to WebEOC alone
- Process explanation for the late identification of the Respond52 exclusion
- Explicit treatment of the frontline pathway under the recommended solution
- Ongoing Ops App cost and risk presented as a downside of the recommendation
- Strategic and opportunity-cost analysis of vendor dependency
- Reconciliation between the 12-month timeline and the original five-year feature-complete position

If any of these are outstanding in V1.2, the questions stand.
