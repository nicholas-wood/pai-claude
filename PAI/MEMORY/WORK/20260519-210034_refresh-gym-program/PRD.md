### Risks

- **Shoulder impingement is real and recurring** — explicitly flagged twice in logs. Continuing high-volume pressing + lateral raises without addressing scapular/thoracic mobility will worsen it. Mobility set MUST include thoracic + scapular work, not just lower body.
- **3x12 ceiling effect** — six weeks at the same rep range with most sets hitting 12 means CNS is no longer the limiter; weight is. Either push the weight harder within 8-12, or open a heavier rep range on compounds. Cutting to 2 sets is wrong direction for stated goals.
- **Anterior pelvic tilt fix is multi-component** — stretching hip flexors alone won't fix it. Need: (a) lengthen hip flexors + lumbar erectors, (b) strengthen glutes + deep abs (transverse abdominis), (c) practice the posterior pelvic tilt motor pattern.
- **Captains Chair after impingement flag** — Captains Chair uses arm pads not a dead hang, so it's safer than hanging leg raise. Should be tolerated, but monitor.
- **Toe-touch goal is slow** — true straight-leg toe-touch is a 8-12 week project for someone with tight posterior chain. Set expectation correctly.

### Plan

**Rep scheme evolution:**
- Compounds (Squat, RDL, Bench, Row, Pulldown, OHP, Incline Press): shift to 4 x 6-8 heavier. Lower reps, more sets, more strength stimulus, drives hypertrophy via tension. This is the natural next phase after 6 weeks of 3x12.
- Secondary compounds (Machine Press, Cable Row, Hack Squat, Leg Press): 3 x 8-10 moderate-heavy.
- Isolation (lateral raises, curls, tri extensions, leg curls, calves, rear delt): stay 3 x 12-15. Isolation work responds to volume + metabolic stress, not load.
- Ab block: 3 x 8-15 depending on exercise (weighted lower, bodyweight higher).
- **Do not drop to 2 sets on compounds.** Stay 3-4 sets where it matters; 2 sets is appropriate only for second-frequency-hit accessories.

**Abs distribution (4 sessions, 2 ab exercises per session, each exercise hit 2x/week):**
- Push: Decline Weighted Situp + Pallof Press
- Pull: Captains Chair + Ab Roller
- Upper: Decline Weighted Situp + Pallof Press
- S&A: Captains Chair + Ab Roller
- Legs: no abs (squat/RDL load core enough)

**Mobility/stretch set (daily, 8-12 min post-workout):**
Every day gets a 3-block structure:
1. Anterior pelvic tilt fix (hip flexor lengthening + glute activation)
2. Posterior chain (toe-touch work — hamstrings, calves, lumbar)
3. Day-specific add (thoracic on press days, lats on pull days, etc.)

## Decisions

- **Why 4x6-8 not 3x5 strength block:** Nick's stated goal is lean muscle + definition, not powerlifting peak. 6-8 keeps hypertrophy stimulus while raising load enough to push past the 3x12 plateau. 4 sets maintains volume; if dropped to 3 sets at lower reps total volume falls too much for hypertrophy.
- **Why not 2 sets:** Hypertrophy literature (Schoenfeld, Helms) consistently shows 10-20 weekly sets per muscle as the sweet spot. Most muscles in current program sit at 11-14 sets/week across 2 frequencies. Halving sets per session would drop weekly volume below threshold.
- **Why 2 ab exercises per session not 1:** User asked for 4 exercises across 4 sessions. Splitting into 1-per-session means each exercise hits once weekly = inadequate frequency. 2 per session lets each exercise hit twice weekly, which is the documented optimum for ab hypertrophy.
- **Why no abs on legs day:** RDL 3110 at 100 kg + back squat at 100 kg already create high axial core load. Adding a direct ab block on legs day extends session and adds CNS load with low marginal benefit.
- **Why couch stretch + glute bridge on same day:** Reciprocal inhibition — stretching the hip flexor reduces neural tone, immediately activating the glute opposite gives the brain a "use this not that" signal. Pairing is more effective than either alone.
