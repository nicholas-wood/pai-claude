Cover-letter rules Nick converged on across the Virgin Australia application (June 2026), beyond what WRITINGSTYLE.md already captures:

- Lead with a thesis/point of view, not a fit-statement. Approved opener: "The thing I have found about AI in practice is that it changes what one person can do." Rejected: "that sits squarely with the role at X" (telling them about their own role), abstract "where my work has landed" framings.
- No skill/stack checklist paragraphs — they read tick-box. The resume carries JD keywords; the letter carries narrative. BUT check keyword coverage at the application level after cutting: the Virgin cuts removed MCP/RAG/evaluation from both documents simultaneously.
- Referral goes in the why-company paragraph organically ("X spoke highly of the company, and his judgment is one I trust"), never as the opening hook.
- Past tense for ended roles ("In my time at ANZ Plus I took a lead role") — Nick flags present-perfect for roles he has left.
- Don't name role location/relocation in the letter; that's a conversation for later stages.
- No semicolons (in addition to the standing no-em-dash rule).
- For orgs that won't know SLSA/LIMSOC: "product manager for a mission-critical incident management system used across the country."

**Why:** Six revision rounds on the Virgin letter; each rule above was an explicit correction or approval.

**How to apply:** Any cover letter or application narrative drafted for Nick. Cross-check [[gotcha-word-pdf-ligature-ats]] before the PDF goes out.
